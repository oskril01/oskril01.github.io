Undergraduate Honors Thesis – Tufts University (2025)

Title: Data Augmentation Models of Stem Cell Growth for Machine Learning Architecture Design
Committee: Emmanuel Tzanakakis (Advisor), James Michael Murphy, Nathanial Eagan
Defended with Highest Honors

  • Manuscript under review – Biotechnology and Bioengineering
  • Oral Presentation – Speaker, AIChE Annual Meeting 2025
  • Poster Presentation – Cell Culture Engineering XIX Conference

This project introduces a technique for evaluating how physics-informed machine learning architectures align with expected biological behavior. By repeatedly sampling trained models through data augmentation and incorporating a meta-learning stage, the approach reveals how different architectures treat key variables across dynamic systems. We demonstrate the method using stem cell growth, identifying which models best capture the impact of glucose and lactate dynamics on pluripotency and viable cell count. This framework offers a generalizable strategy for model selection in any coupled dynamical system, including applications in metabolic engineering, tissue modeling, and predictive biomanufacturing.
